ONLINE  DEFAMATION

DOn't be a troll it isn't worth the toll

About

If someone causes harm to your reputation by publishing material about you that changes the way people feel about you, you may have been harmed in a legal sense. Not all offensive, embarrassing or upsetting remarks cause harm to your reputation.

Broadly, defamation is the spreading of false information which damages someones reputation. Defamation in an online context is difficult to define and even more difficult to police. As easy to see, the general definition of defamation applies directly to online communities that are populated by trolls, cyberbullies and catfishers.

One of difficulties with traditional defamation understandings is that they are based in an understanding that they are based in an understanding that the originator of the false information is easily identifiable , however in a online world this can be difficult

DEFENCE 1

IF THE COMMENTS ARE PROVEN TO BE SUBSTANTIALLY TRUE

If the comments made by defamer are proven to be factual  

DEFENCE 2

IF THE COMMENTS ARE PUBLISHED UNDER LEGAL PRIVELAGE

Privilege occurs in situations when the law protects people's rights to express themselves, such as a speech in parliament, or statements made in a court of law.

DEFENCE 3

FAIR REPORTING

If the publication involves fair reporting about proceeding of public concern or from documents that are already publicly available.

DEFENCE 4

THE COMMENTS ARER PARTLY PROTECTED BY PRIVELAGE   

If the publication is partly protected by privilege, such as making reasonable comments about work performance to a potential employer as a referee.
