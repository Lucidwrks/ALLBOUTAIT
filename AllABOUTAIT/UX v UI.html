UX versus UI

HOw ARE THEY SIMILAR? HOW ARE THEY DIFFERENT?

About

The UX (User Experience) is the process of enhancing customer satisfaction and loyalty by improving the usability, ease of use, and pleasure provided in the interaction between the customer and the product. 

The foundation of the UX is basedon multiple factors: The visual design, information architecture, information, structure, organisation, labelling,  usability, accessibility and inclusivity.

The UI (User Interface) is the space where interaction between humans and technology occurs. The goal of this interaction is effective operation and control of the technology from the user's point of view 

About

Key factors that imapact the effectiveness of the UI are it's consistency, how easy it is to navigte and how functional it is.       

The UI must also be logical and hierarchal to reduce the clutter of the produc, improve how the digital product flows and to have important information that the user needs and wants to access first is focal in the digital product.     

For the best digital product, the UI and UX work in harmony with each other along with effective use of the design elements and design principles   

Difference 1 

THE UX IS FOCUSED ON THE USERS JOURNERY WITH THE DIGITAL PRODUCT

A UX designer would think about how the user will feel when using the produt and how this feeling can be maintained in a positive state so the user will continue to use the product    

Differemce 2

THE UI ON THE OTHER HAND IS FOCUSED ON  THE METHODS AND STRUCTURES AMD STRUCTURES WHICH A USER INTERACTS WITH

A UI designer will think about how the physical structure and elements will keep the user satisfied.  Tools such as site map can help a UI designer to establish a clear and effective structure     

SIMILARITY 1

 USER SATISFACTION IS THE ULTIMATE GOAL

Both need to  implement features to please their users and create user-friendly products. The UX and UI complement each other and make a project look alluring.

SIMILARITY 2

BOTH ARE AWARE OF THE NEEDS OF A USER

 Both need to consider factors of usabilit, accessibilty and inclusivity  when a digital product is being created if the product is going to please the consumer.

UX: VISUAL DESIGN

How does the digital product implement the design principles and elements in it's final product?  

UX: Information and Information Architecture

How is the information organised? How is the information being delievered in the digital product?  

UX: Srucuturing, Organising and Labelling 

Structuring: Is the infromation reduced to its basics and key points? Is the information related to each other?

Organising: Is the information grouped in a appropriate manner?

Labelling: Is the wording used appropriate. does it suit the language used by the target audience? are labels used appropriately and effectively?      

 

UX: Usability and Accessability

usability: Can the Digital product be used by clients to acheive their goals? will it achieve such goals in a effective manner?

Accesibility: is the product able to be accessed by its intended users? even those with dissabilities and impairments? 

UI: Consistency

Does the digital product have consistency between the various components of the technology it uses?   

UI: Navigable 

Does the UI use navigation controls effectively? is the product intuitive for the client and can the  easily navigate it? 

UI: Functionality

Does the product serve the purpose for it's intended target audience

UI: Usability, Accesibility and Inclusivity

Usability:Do the features of the UI allow the client to get their tasks done in a effetive manner? Is the UI simple to follow?

Accesibilty: What features are present that allows people who are disabled or have impairments. how will these features help these people?

Inclusivity: In regards to the Graphical User Interface, how does the digital product account for different cultural groups? Is there content that may offend people? Are Gender Neutral colours over used?
