About

IP rights provide IP owners with the time and opportunity to commercialise their creations. This protection serves as an incentive to innovate.The creator of the IP is not necesarily the owner. IP ownership can be agreed upon through apropriate contractual arrangements, especially with employees, distributors and manufacturors.

- IP rights exist in many forms. In some cases they don't need to be registered in order to be of value. Each type of IP provides different competitive advantages.

Registering a business, company or domain name does not give you exclusive rights like registered IP does. If you register a business, company or domain name, you do not automatically have the right to use that name as a trade mark.

PATENT

Patent IP protects the individuals inventions and new processes for example, polymer banknotes.      

TRADEMARKS

Trademarks protect logos, words, letters, numbers, colours, phrases, sounds, scents, shapes, pictures, aspects of packaging or branding-or any combination of these. This identifies the particular goods or services of a trader as distinct from those of other traders. For example the vegemite logo or O'brien jingle

Registered Design

Pretects the visual appearance of a product but not the way it works. For example footwear, fashion items and kitchen appliances

Plant Breeder's Rights

Protects new plant varieties. Plant breeder's rights protect the commercial rights of new plant variaties. For example cotton plants with insect resistance and the pink iceberg rose

Geographical Indication

Identifies a good as originating in a specific territory, region or locality where a particular quality, reputation or other characteristic is essentially attributable to its geographical origin Australia has a dual system for protecting GIs –certification trade marks can be used to protect GIs for all goods, while GIs for wine can be protected on a standalone wine register. For example Stilton Cheese

 

 

Copyright

Copyright protects drawings, art, literature, music, film, broadcasts, computer programs. The owner's original expression of ideas is protected, but not the ideas themselves.

Trade Secrets

Protects any confidential information, including secret formulas, processes, and methods used in production.These types of IP rights give creators certain rights and privileges depending on the type of IP protection.Coca-Cola has used trade secrets to keep its formula from becoming public for decades

 

 

Circuit Layouts

Protects layout designs or plans of integrated circuits used in computer-generated designs. Similar to copyright, the owner’s original layout design is protected, but they have a unique form of protection. Computer chips or semi-conductor chip designs in pacemakers and PCs
